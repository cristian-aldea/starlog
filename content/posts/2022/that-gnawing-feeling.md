Fair warning, this post will cover a topic that's heavy and personal. I'm writing this to articulate something that's been on my mind for a few weeks, and to help anyone who might be going through something similar.

Feel free to reach out and talk to me if you feel the need.

Don't worry, however. I have more posts in the works about programming and other topics I hope to share soon. Stay tuned for that :)

## A Feeling

For the longest time, when I was alone with my thoughts, I'd start noticing a painful feeling of tension in my chest. It was always present, and I often didn't notice it.

For years I'd drown out this feeling, as many of us might do; through school, work, games, food or other distractions.

This feeling would become unbearable sometimes, and I didn't know where it came from, or what to do with it.

## Anxiety and Depression

We all have our inner thoughts. Despite my best efforts, my thoughts were often cruel, harmful and unproductive.

In my early childhood and teen years, I didn't have the most supportive environment, and I went through some painful experiences that changed me for the worse.

Later in life, I met good people. However, I was scared to be myself. I would freeze up, monitor my every action, afraid of being judged.

This vicious cycle spiralled during the pandemic and the cold dark months of winter. Every mistake was amplified. Every achievement was meaningless. Every bad experience was catastrophic. This feeling exhausted me, isolated me, unable to positively deal with the world.

## Understanding and Connection

Then, I had a few lucky breaks. I got to have some fun conversations. For a second, I stopped thinking about myself and started listening to others. I started to understand them a little better. I then opened up too about how I was feeling, and felt a little better.

By sharing and connecting, the pain in my chest subsided, and I started to introspect.

I reflected on my life so far, the good and the bad. Previously, I'd only feel shame and embarrassment when thinking back on what my life was. Now, not so much. With context, I understand my past self better, and how I became who I am. For a moment, my inner thoughts didn't drag me down. I wanted to be better, but I no longer felt bad about who I was.

## Moving forward

Despite the dramatic way I've written everything so far, my life isn't suddenly perfect. I'm still me, still susceptible to relapse when things get tough again.

But moving forward, I'll work on being ok with myself, because it's the least I deserve. I've realized one important thing recently; connection and understanding are one of the best things we can experience in our lives. We have some control over how we choose to engage with the world. The energy I spent fighting myself, I'll try my best to direct it positively.

If you've ever felt that **gnawing feeling**, feel free to reach out.
