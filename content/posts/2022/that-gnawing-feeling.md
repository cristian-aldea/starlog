Fair warning, this post will cover a topic that's heavy and personal. I'm writing this to articulate something that's been on my mind for a few weeks, and to help anyone who might be going through something similar.

Feel free to reach out and talk to me if you feel the need.

Don't worry however, I have more posts in the works about programming and other topics I hope to share soon. Stay tuned for that :)

## A Feeling

For the longest time, when I was alone with my thoughts, I'd start noticing a painful feeling of tension in my chest. It was always present, and I often didn't notice it.

For years I'd drown out this feeling, through school, work, games, food or other distractions.

This feeling would become unbearable sometimes, and I didn't know where it came from, or what to do with it.

## Anxiety

We all have our inner thoughts. Despite my best efforts, my thoughts were often cruel, harmful and unproductive.

In my early childhood and teen years, I went through some painful experiences that changed me for the worse.

Later in life, I met good people. But even with these wonderful people, I'd be scared to be myself. I would freeze up, monitor my every action, afraid of being judged.

This vicious cycle spiralled during the pandemic and the cold dark months of winter. Every mistake was amplified. Every achievement was meaningless. Every bad experience was catastrophic. This made me tired, unable to deal with the world in a positive way.

## Understanding

Then, I had a few conversations. For a second, I stopped thinking about myself and started listening to others. Suddenly, I felt like I understood them a bit better. When I opened up too, I also felt understood.

By sharing my pain with someone else, I felt a connection, and could then connect with myself a bit better.

I realized many things about myself. For a moment, I didn't have that gnawing feeling anymore. For a moment, my inner thoughts didn't bring me pain. They were compassionate too. For that moment, I still wanted to be better, but I no longer felt bad about who I was.

## Moving forward

Despite this, my life isn't suddenly magically better. I'm still the same person, still flawed, still able to make mistakes.

But moving forward, I'll work on being ok with myself, because its the least I deserve. The energy I spent fighting myself, I'll try my best to direct it positively.

If you've ever felt that **gnawing feeling**, I'll do my best to listen.
